Sillicon Valley

California, State, western U.S. Area: 163,695 sq mi (423,967 sq km). Population: (2020) 39,538,223; (2022 est.) 39,029,342. Capital: Sacramento. The state is bordered by Mexico, Oregon, Nevada, and Arizona and is the largest in population and third largest in area in the U.S. It features both the highest and lowest points in the 48 contiguous states. The region's history dates back to its original Native American inhabitants and later Spanish and Mexican control before becoming part of the U.S. in 1848. The 1848 gold rush spurred settlement, and California's population grew significantly in the 20th century, supported by its large economy and occasionally challenged by severe earthquakes.
